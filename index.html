Current Tours

Current British Columbia tour, including a performance by New Orleans Jazz in London. The show in Toronto drew close to 300 people by October for the band during the festival. "The crowd definitely wanted to show some more people, but we decided that we hadn't been able to have a long time in Canada that day because this place was such a dark place," said Mr. J. Scott, the frontman and music teacher. The concert also hosted a "Festival of the Sacred" in Calgary this fall to raise money for cancer research. The concert provided financial support for health care for over 1,000 hospital patients. After performing in Montreal, the new Canadian tour will continue in March in Ottawa.

Past Tours

Past British Columbia Canada tours have made a big difference for the local artists. The B.C. Museum is now at 1,500 metres ahead of schedule and will be offering three tours this year. "It's always good to see when people are talking about the work of artists here," says David Brownley, who runs St. Joseph's B.C. Inuit Art Museum. "But when the artists and the community get together, they're on the right side of history." Past British Columbia Canada tours and conventions is only available on one page.
